# The questions under the ramble

Dee's transcripts look like chaos. They aren't. Underneath the steam and the aunties there are eight questions being answered, and they are the only eight that matter before anyone touches a colour.

Answer them by talking, not typing. Record a voice memo for each, one minute minimum, then transcribe the lot. If an answer fits in one sentence, you haven't answered yet, keep talking. Adjectives are banned: "professional, modern, clean" tells a designer nothing. Stories, rants and refusals tell them everything.

## The eight

**1. The moment.**
Tell the story of the exact moment you knew this was your thing. Where were you standing, what did it smell like, what could you hear?
*In Dee's run: the 7am wet market, fish on ice, uncles shouting. That one answer set the whole palette temperature.*

**2. One real person.**
Describe one actual person you serve. Their Tuesday. What they're quietly embarrassed about. What they'd never admit they want.
*In Dee's run: the Raffles Place kid who orders delivery every night and has never spoken to a hawker uncle.*

**3. The refusals.**
Finish this sentence out loud, three times, angrier each time: "If my brand ever looks like ......, I will ......"
*In Dee's run: "if my brand ever looks like a moody dark restaurant ad I want someone to slap me." The never-list wrote itself.*

**4. Three feelings, earned.**
What three feelings should a stranger get in the first five seconds? Then, for each one, tell a sixty-second story that proves you actually create that feeling. No story, no word.
*In Dee's run: fun, loud, honest, each backed by a memory, not a mood board.*

**5. The flop that felt wrong.**
The piece of work you made that felt off even before it failed. What exactly was wrong with it?
*In Dee's run: his one attempt at cinematic slow-mo food footage. It flopped in numbers and in his gut, and it ruled out an entire aesthetic.*

**6. The table you'd share.**
Two or three accounts or brands you'd happily sit next to. Say precisely what they get right, in your own words, and name one thing of theirs you would never copy.
*In Dee's run: "everything just feels turned up and warm, not this washed out muted aesthetic everyone's doing now." That line is a palette brief.*

**7. Your room.**
Describe the physical place where your work is most itself, at its best hour. The light, the noise, the temperature, what's on the walls.
*In Dee's run: the market at dawn under stall lights. The identity's colours are that room's colours.*

**8. The sleep test.**
What work would you turn down even if it paid well? Why?
*In Dee's run: anything "atas". The refusal drew the line his whole brand stands behind.*
