# The derive prompt

Copy everything below the line into a capable AI, then paste your transcripts underneath it. This is the same discipline the blind agent followed when it scored five for five on Dee.

---

You are deriving a visual direction from interview transcripts. The transcripts are the only source of truth. Work from evidence, never from taste alone.

## Part 1: voice evidence

Before any design thinking, extract:

- How this person talks: pace, sentence shape, energy, formality
- A phrase bank: 10 short verbatim quotes that carry their voice
- Tone words: the feelings they want people to get, verbatim where stated
- Who they serve and how they position against their niche
- What they refuse to be, quoted, vivid
- Any visual preferences they stated themselves: accounts they admire, work of theirs that performed, work that flopped

## Part 2: derived visual direction

From that evidence only:

1. **Palette temperature first.** Decide hot or cool, bright or deep, saturated or muted, and say why, citing quotes. Rough compass: raw, bold, fast talkers run dark and saturated; warm, trusted, calm ones run light and earthy; loud, playful ones run bright and hot. Then, and only then, propose 3 or 4 concrete hex values with roles (base, primary, secondary, accent). Each hex gets one line of evidence: "they said X."
2. **Type pairing.** One display face, one body face, both available on Google Fonts. Fast punchy talkers take tight high-contrast display faces; slow warm storytellers take rounder generous ones. The body face must survive their actual copy. One optional mono if numbers carry their proof.
3. **Layout grammar.** Two or three sentences on how a page should be organised, tied to how they actually talk. If they're calm, air is your material. If they're loud, air may be the enemy. Follow their energy, not a house style.
4. **The banned list.** Three to five things this brand must never look like, each traced to one of their refusals.
5. **The feeling.** One sentence: what a stranger should feel in the first five seconds on their page.

## Rules

- Maximum 2 typefaces plus the optional mono. Maximum 3 colours plus one accent.
- Every single pick traces to a quote or a stated preference. If you can't cite it, change the pick.
- Where evidence is thin or contradictory, say so plainly and mark that choice "react and adjust." Do not paper over gaps with confidence.
- No design cliches from their industry unless their own words demand it.

Return the full report in structured markdown: Part 1 evidence, then Part 2 direction.
