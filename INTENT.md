## Intended palette temperature
**Bright + saturated + warm.** Not pastel-bright, not neon-cold. Think market-stall energy: warm yellows/reds/oranges with room for one loud accent (could read as a warm pink, chili red, turmeric yellow, or similar) — never desaturated, never moody-dark, never monochrome-minimal. Evidence: "washed out muted aesthetic" explicitly rejected (Q9, re: @loudkakis contrast); "warm chaos" (Q5); "brighter and louder" (Q7); "keep it loud, keep it warm" (Q26); rejection of "moody dark restaurant ad" (Q5).

## Intended typography energy
**Loud, condensed, high-impact display type** for headlines/wordmark — something with shouting, poster/signage energy (condensed sans or bold display face, the kind of type that could sit on a hand-painted market signboard or a loud street poster) — paired with a **friendly, approachable, slightly informal body typeface** for actual reading (should not feel corporate, should not feel like a fine-dining menu serif, should not feel sterile/geometric-luxury). Evidence: "if I sound like I'm reading an ad I've already failed" (Q5) rules out slick/corporate polish; "I want people to feel the volume before they even read a word" (Q21) directly implies type/graphics need to shout before content is read; the physical-chaos description in Q24 (shouting, clanging, unscripted) implies energetic, not restrained, letterforms.

## Three things the design must NEVER be
1. **Atas / minimal-luxury** — no fine-dining elevation, no tweezer-plating aesthetic, no minimalist lifestyle-brand look. Direct quote: "if the branding ever looks like some minimalist luxury lifestyle brand I think I'd actually feel sick."
2. **Corporate / polished-agency** — no "curated culinary journey" sheen, nothing that reads like a scripted ad or a slick B2B deck. Direct quote: "if I sound like I'm reading an ad I've already failed" + "no corporate speak."
3. **Food-porn moody-dark** — no slow-mo glossy close-ups, no sad-piano dark restaurant ad lighting, no desaturated/washed-out muted aesthetic. Direct quote: "if my content or my brand ever looks like a moody dark restaurant ad I want someone to slap me" + confirmed by his own flop story (Q18) where he tried this style and it failed both metrically and emotionally.

## One-sentence feeling a visitor should get
A visitor should land on the page and immediately feel like they've walked into a bright, noisy, warm wet-market morning with a loud friend pulling them by the arm — not like they've opened a quiet, tasteful lifestyle catalogue.
