The three words

His own three words, offered unprompted. Everything else bends to these.

FUN

"genuinely had fun, not 'educational' fun, actual laughing fun, because half my class is just me and the aunties trash talking each other while cooking."

"oh, this is going to be fun, this is not serious, this is not a museum tour of hawker food."

– Dee, Q7 & Q21

LOUD

"I want people to leave feeling like their senses got turned up, everything brighter and louder and more alive, the opposite of how a Tuesday at their desk job feels."

"I want people to feel the volume before they even read a word."

– Dee, Q7 & Q21

HONEST

"real, unfiltered, nothing was staged for them, they saw actual market chaos actual auntie sass actual mess."

"keep it a little rough around the edges, that roughness is the whole point."

– Dee, Q7 & Q26

The scrawl treatment (no third font)

Faked with rotated, underlined italic Familjen Grotesk. like a marker note on the wall

Why: Wei Jie's line – Dee's refuse-list should be "on the wall of the kitchen." The scrawl is that wall-scribble, not a calligraphy font.

05
Layout & photography

White space is the enemy. Packed, overlapping, loud. Rough edges are the point.

Grammar: oversized headlines that bleed off-frame, elements that collide slightly, captions stacked like phone-shot stories. Traces to: "keep it a little rough around the edges, that roughness is the whole point."

Pack it, overlap it

Blocks touch and collide. Tags rotate. Fill the frame like a stall counter at lunch rush. If it looks calm, it's wrong.

Phone-flash-flat photo

Bright, raw, on-camera flash. Flat and honest, the way the wet market actually looks at 7am. Never moody, never graded down.

DO

Bright direct flash on wet fish, steam, sweaty faces mid-laugh. Overexposed and alive. "It's not a pretty smell necessarily but it's alive."

DON'T

Moody dark restaurant lighting, glossy dripping-sauce close-up. "if my content or my brand ever looks like a moody dark restaurant ad I want someone to slap me."

DO

Headline crashing off the edge, three tags stacked and rotated, caption over image like a re-shared story. Loud cousin filming on his phone.

DON'T

Airy minimalist grid, one centred dish, oceans of white margin. "if the branding ever looks like some minimalist luxury lifestyle brand I think I'd actually feel sick."

DO

Slightly ugly char kway teow shot proudly, plated like a Michelin meal anyway. Real mess, real plates, real hands.

DON'T

Slow-mo food porn, sad piano, tweezers and small plates. "hawker food doesn't need elevating, it's already at the top."

How to read this doc: every colour, type, and layout call above states its reason, traced to Dee's own words from the discovery memos. The doc is built IN the brand it describes – hot field, packed layout, Anton shout + Familjen body, zero white-minimal ground. Chips are marked react & adjust: the direction is locked, the exact shades are proposed and open to his gut reaction.

Tension move: the cover wordmark "DEE" is set at 640px and deliberately bleeds off the left edge with the middle E flipped to cream, so it reads half-broken on purpose. Losing part of the name is the risky bit – it trusts the volume over legibility, exactly what he asked for: feel it before you read it.
