# Brand Voice Notes — Dee (Deepak Nair) — Distilled from Discovery Memos

Working notes pulled from the raw voice memo transcripts. For internal use ahead of visual direction work.

## Origin / why
- Grandma took him to Chinatown wet market from age 4 — "watching his hands, so fast, and the steam and the shouting" — formative memory of noise + food + being small in a big loud place.
- Started filming hawker eats broke, in poly, almost by accident.
- Reframed his own mission mid-interview: "I wasn't just doing food content anymore I was basically translating... hawker culture back to people who grew up next to it and never actually touched it."
- Self-description: "I'm not a chef... I'm just the guy who'll stand in the queue with you and go 'okay this is how you do it.'"

## Who he serves
- NOT tourists, NOT expats chasing "cultural experience."
- Explicit target: "the twenty-five year old Singaporean who works in some office in Raffles Place... has literally never spoken to a hawker stall uncle... secretly feels a bit embarrassed that they don't know their own food culture."
- Intent: gently needle the guilt, then release it — "this is yours, you're allowed to love this."

## What he refuses to be (his own numbered list, verbatim energy)
1. **Atas** — "the moment this feels like some ang moh fine dining 'elevated hawker experience' with small plates and tweezers I am OUT."
2. **Scripted influencer polish** — "if I sound like I'm reading an ad I've already failed."
3. **Slow-motion food porn** — "no glossy dripping-sauce close-up with sad piano music... it's LOUD... chaos and it's warm chaos... if my content or my brand ever looks like a moody dark restaurant ad I want someone to slap me."
4. **Corporate speak** — "say that to a hawker uncle and see what happens to you lah."
- Reinforced later: "don't make this look expensive lah... if the branding ever looks like some minimalist luxury lifestyle brand I think I'd actually feel sick."

## Desired feeling (his own three words, offered unprompted)
- **Fun** — "genuinely had fun... actual laughing fun."
- **Loud** — "I want people to leave feeling like their senses got turned up, everything brighter and louder and more alive."
- **Honest** — "real, unfiltered, nothing was staged."
- His summary: "fun, loud, honest. that's it that's the whole thing."

## Reference accounts (admired energy)
- @noknowledgeneeded — "chaotic energy, doesn't over produce anything, feels like your loud cousin filming on his phone."
- @auntieapproved — warm, funny, small (~20k), never exploitative of the aunties.
- @loudkakis — "very bright colours, very in your face... everything just feels turned up and warm, not this washed out muted aesthetic everyone's doing now." (Closest to a direct temperature reference.)

## Proof / best content
- Blindfolded hawker-tasting video: 4.2M views, unplanned emotional moment over fishball soup (grandma callback). Comments: "made me cry over fishball soup."
- Business trajectory: started at 6/10 seats filled at $68/class → now $128 standard / $188 deluxe, selling out within hours, July batch (12 slots) gone in 40 minutes with 30+ on waitlist.
- Learned lesson from a flop: tried cinematic slow-pan/moody food-porn style once — flopped AND felt wrong to him personally. Confirms rule #3 above isn't just taste, it's tested.

## First-impression goal
- "I want people to feel the volume before they even read a word." Explicitly rejects calm/minimal/quiet-studio impressions.

## Texture / physical world detail (useful for imagery)
- 7am wet market, stall lights, ice, shouting aunties, chaotic kitchen, burning garlic, everyone sweating and laughing.
- Recurring cast: Auntie Poh Choo (adopted him), his mum (skeptic-turned-supporter), Wei Jie (business partner).

## Voice/tone patterns (how he talks, for copywriting reference)
- Singlish inflections: "lah," "ah," "wah," "atas."
- Self-interrupting, tangential, corrects himself mid-thought.
- Numbers things out loud when making a point ("number one... number two...").
- Repeats key words for emphasis rather than using fancier vocabulary.
